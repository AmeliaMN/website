Simultaneous contrast:

The first study demonstrates that the same hue will appear darker or lighter depending on what ground it is upon.


The second study demonstrates the visual change in saturation that occurs with grounds of different saturations.


The third study changes the warmth of the figure color depending on the warmth of the ground color.


The fourth study uses two different figure colors and attempts a visual "sameness" by the manipulation of the ground colors.

Optical Color Mix:

The objective of this project was to select five colors, and using the principles laid out by Josef Albers, manipulate their appearance by organization. Observe that the first row appears more orange, the second yellower, the third greener and the fourth more purple. This is simply an optical effect. The colors appear on their own at the bottom of the figure.
