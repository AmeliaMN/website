Vectors and matrices

vec <- c(1, 2, 3, 10, 100)
vec
## [1]   1   2   3  10 100
mat <- matrix(c(1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6), nrow = 2)
mat
##      [,1] [,2] [,3]
## [1,]    1    3    5
## [2,]    2    4    6
matrix(c(1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6), nrow = 2)
##      [,1] [,2] [,3]
## [1,]    1    3    5
## [2,]    2    4    6
matrix(c(1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6), nrow = 3)

## [1,]    1    4
## [2,]    2    5

Math with vectors and matrices

vec + 4
## [1]   5   6   7  14 104
vec * 4
## [1]   4   8  12  40 400
vec * vec # element-wise multiplication
## [1]     1     4     9   100 10000
vec %*% vec # matrix multiplication (inner)
##       [,1]
## [1,] 10114
vec %o% vec # matrix multiplication (outer)
##      [,1] [,2] [,3] [,4]  [,5]
## [1,]    1    2    3   10   100
## [2,]    2    4    6   20   200
## [3,]    3    6    9   30   300
## [4,]   10   20   30  100  1000
## [5,]  100  200  300 1000 10000
mat
##      [,1] [,2] [,3]
## [1,]    1    3    5
## [2,]    2    4    6
t(mat) # transpose
##      [,1] [,2]
## [1,]    1    2
## [2,]    3    4
## [3,]    5    6

Beatles data

Let’s make a toy dataset to play with.

beatles <- data.frame(
  name = c("John", "Paul", "George", "Ringo"),
  birth = c(1940, 1942, 1943, 1940), 
  instrument = c("guitar", "bass", "guitar", "drums")
)

Select

Select is a way to extract columns from dataframes

select(beatles, name, birth)
##     name birth
## 1   John  1940
## 2   Paul  1942
## 3 George  1943
## 4  Ringo  1940
